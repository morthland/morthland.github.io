THE IDEA

I went through a crazy phase researching and learning all I could about space. While on this journey, the 44th anniversary of the Apollo 11 moon landing was nearing. During this time I also was looking to test out some new coding and design ideas that I had brewing. So I decided to create a website that followed this epic journey to the moon. Trying out some new techniques, while providing insight into the best thing that has ever happened. Walking on the freakin moon!

FEATURES & BENEFITS

I had read, watched, and consumed tons of information on the space program. I compiled a list of resources and research about the event and slowly formed the information architecture for the site. After a rough outline of the site and its features, I created elements and illustrations to show various aspects of the mission. I created custom CCS3 animations to illustrate the journey the spaceship would embarq.

KEY FEATURES

- Custom made CCS3 animations

- Scroll and stick main navigation

- Adaptive resolution image gallery

- Parallaxing background

- Youtube video scroller wall

- Information and relevant content to the event

THE PROCESS

Playbook started life as a rough prototype application created by a few backend developers. This version was focused strictly on funtionality. There was no true designer or UX personel assigned to the Phase I funding. The result was a functioning applicatons that was almost impossible to use. When phase II began I was brought on board to completely redesign and improve the user experience.

With the Phase I version as a starting point, I completely overhauled the Visual, UI, and UX of the application. Following the Android design guidelines to fit within the standard and excepted standards for modern tablet designs.

The application was originally developed to run in portrait mode. This format was not ideal for many of the actions completed within the app. By Phase III of the project, I converted the layout to landscape to accomidate actions the user takes. Switching the scene builder to a 16:9 landscape aspect ratio allowed the scene to be formated correctly for when it was time to publish and share the final video/play. While also allowing for better action pane and white space managment.

Working directly with developers, project managers, subject matter experts and the client themselves, the application was designed to be quick, easy, and intuitive while also being a complex tool.

The Result

This project was an excellent execution of research, practice, and passion. I was looking to get stumped, looking to dig myself out of a hard design issue by teaching myself along the way. I was very pleased with the outcome and feadback I recieved. The space website Universe Today featured my site and brought a steady stream of visitors during launch week. Along with traffic and feedback from the reddit community.
